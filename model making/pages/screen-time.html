Screen Time Limits

Managing how long you spend on devices can improve focus, sleep, and wellbeing. Setting limits helps you stay in control instead of being pulled in by apps and notifications.

Why set limits?

  • Apps and platforms are designed to keep you engaged. Without limits, usage can grow without you noticing.
  • Excessive screen time is linked to poorer sleep, eye strain, and less time for offline activities.
  • Intentional limits free time for rest, relationships, and hobbies.

Built-in tools

  • iOS: Screen Time in Settings lets you set app limits, downtime, and content restrictions.
  • Android: Digital Wellbeing (or similar) shows usage and allows app timers and focus mode.
  • Browsers: Extensions can block or limit time on specific sites.

Practical steps

Decide how much time per day is reasonable for social media, games, or video. Set a limit slightly below that so you have a buffer. Schedule "no phone" blocks (e.g. during meals or the first hour after waking). Put your phone in another room at night to support better sleep. Review your usage weekly and adjust limits as needed.
