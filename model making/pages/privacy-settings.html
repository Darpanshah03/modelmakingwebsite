Privacy Settings

Most apps and services have privacy settings that control who sees your information and how your data is used. Reviewing and tightening these settings reduces exposure and misuse.

What to check

  • Profile visibility: Who can see your profile, posts, and story? Prefer "Friends" or "Only me" over "Public" where you don't need reach.
  • Contact discovery: Restrict who can find you by phone number or email if you don't want strangers adding you.
  • Tagging and mentions: Control who can tag you or mention you, and who can see posts you're tagged in.
  • Ad preferences: Limit ad personalisation and data shared with advertisers where the option exists.

Where to find them

Look for "Privacy", "Security", or "Account" in the app or website settings. Go through each section rather than accepting defaults. Do this for social media, email, cloud storage, and any service that holds personal data.

Ongoing habit

Companies change settings and policies. Recheck privacy settings every few months or after major app updates. When you sign up for a new service, set privacy options before you start using it.
