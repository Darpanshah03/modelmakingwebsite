Healthy Boundaries

Setting boundaries with devices and notifications helps you stay focused, present, and in control of your time and attention.

Notifications

  • Turn off non-essential notifications. Keep only what you truly need (e.g. messages from close contacts, calendar).
  • Use Do Not Disturb or Focus mode during work, study, or family time. Schedule it if your device supports it.
  • Disable sounds and pop-ups for social media and news so you check them when you choose, not when they demand.

Device-free zones

Choose places or times where devices are off or out of reach: e.g. bedroom, dinner table, or the first hour after waking. This improves sleep and connection with others.

Boundaries with others

Tell family or colleagues when you're unavailable. Set expectations (e.g. "I don't check email after 7 p.m."). Model healthy use for children and discuss why limits matter.

Review regularly

Check your notification and app settings every few months. Remove or mute apps that no longer deserve your attention. Adjust boundaries as your routine changes.
