Data You Share

Whenever you sign up, fill a form, or use an app, you share data. Being mindful about what you share and with whom reduces risk and keeps more control in your hands.

What companies collect

Services often collect: name, email, phone, location, purchase history, browsing behaviour, and device information. This can be used for personalisation, ads, analytics, or sold to third parties depending on the privacy policy.

Minimise what you give

  • Only fill in required fields when signing up. Use a separate email for non-essential sign-ups if you want to limit exposure.
  • Don't grant permissions an app doesn't clearly need (e.g. contacts for a flashlight app).
  • Use "Sign in with Apple" or similar where you can hide your email, so you don't hand over your main address to every app.

Read privacy policies

For important services (banking, health, kids' apps), skim the privacy policy: what data they collect, how long they keep it, who they share it with, and your rights (access, deletion, opt-out). If something is unclear or excessive, consider an alternative service.

Assume it's permanent

Assume anything you post or share can be copied, saved, or leaked. Don't share sensitive personal or financial details unless the channel is secure and the recipient is trusted.
