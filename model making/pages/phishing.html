Phishing Awareness

Phishing is when attackers pretend to be a trusted person or organisation to steal passwords, money, or data. It often happens via email, SMS, or messaging apps.

Common signs of phishing

  • Urgency: Messages that say "act now", "account suspended", or "verify immediately" to pressure you.
  • Unexpected requests: Asking for passwords, PINs, or payment details. Legitimate services don't ask for these by email or text.
  • Strange links: Hover over links to see the real URL. Phishing sites often use similar-looking domains (e.g. paypa1.com instead of paypal.com).
  • Attachments: Unexpected or suspicious attachments (e.g. .exe, .zip, Office files) can contain malware.
  • Sender address: The display name can be faked. Check the actual email address, not just the name.

What to do

  • Don't click links or open attachments in suspicious messages. Open the site or app yourself by typing the URL or using a bookmark.
  • Verify by contacting the company through their official website or phone number, not via the message you received.
  • Report phishing to your email provider and to the organisation being impersonated.
  • If you think you clicked a bad link or entered credentials, change that password immediately and enable 2FA if you haven't already.
