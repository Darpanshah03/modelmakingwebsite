Verify Before You Share

False or misleading information spreads quickly online. Sharing without checking can harm others and damage your own credibility.

Why verification matters

  • Misinformation can affect health, safety, and decisions. A single share can reach thousands of people.
  • Deepfakes and edited media make it harder to trust what you see and hear.
  • Stopping the spread starts with pausing before you share.

How to verify

  • Check the source: Is it a known, reputable outlet? Is the account or site impersonating someone else?
  • Look for other coverage: Do trusted news organisations or official bodies report the same thing?
  • Check the date: Old stories or images are sometimes shared as if they're current.
  • Reverse image search: Use Google Images or TinEye to see where an image really came from.
  • Fact-checking sites: Use Snopes, FactCheck.org, or similar for claims that go viral.

When in doubt

Don't share. Ask yourself: "Do I know this is true?" If not, either verify first or skip sharing. Slowing down helps reduce the spread of false information.
