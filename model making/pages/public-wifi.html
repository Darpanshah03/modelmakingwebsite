Public Wi‑Fi Caution

Public Wi‑Fi in cafés, airports, and hotels is convenient but often unencrypted or poorly secured. Others on the same network may be able to see your traffic.

Risks on public Wi‑Fi

  • Eavesdropping: Unencrypted traffic can be read by anyone on the network with simple tools.
  • Fake hotspots: Attackers sometimes set up networks with names like "Airport WiFi" or "Hotel Guest" to capture data.
  • Man-in-the-middle: Your connection can be intercepted so that even "secure" sites are compromised if the network is hostile.

What to avoid

Don't do banking, shopping, or log into important accounts on public Wi‑Fi unless you're using a VPN. Don't enter passwords or personal details on sites that aren't using HTTPS (look for the lock icon). Avoid auto-connecting to open networks; connect only when you choose to.

Using a VPN

A VPN encrypts your traffic between your device and the VPN server, so others on the same Wi‑Fi can't see what you're doing. Choose a reputable VPN with a clear no-logs policy and strong encryption. Use it whenever you're on public or untrusted networks.

Alternative

If you don't use a VPN, prefer your phone's mobile data (4G/5G) for sensitive tasks—it's generally more secure than unknown public Wi‑Fi.
