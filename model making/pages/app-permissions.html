App Permissions

Apps often request access to your camera, microphone, location, contacts, and files. Granting more than needed can risk your privacy and security.

Why permissions matter

  • Some apps collect more data than they need for their core function.
  • Abused or leaked data can be used for tracking, fraud, or harassment.
  • Malicious apps can use permissions to spy or steal information.

Common permissions

  • Location: Needed for maps or local services; not for a simple calculator or flashlight app.
  • Camera & microphone: Needed for video calls or photos; revoke when the app doesn't need them.
  • Contacts: Often used for "find friends"; consider whether you really want to share your contacts.
  • Storage/Files: Needed for file managers or editors; avoid giving broad access if the app only needs specific folders.

What to do

On first install, only allow permissions that are clearly necessary. Review permissions periodically in your device settings (e.g. Settings → Apps → [App] → Permissions). Revoke access you no longer need. Prefer apps from official stores and check reviews and the developer's reputation before installing.
