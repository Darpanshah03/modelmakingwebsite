Digital Breaks

Taking regular breaks from screens reduces eye strain, improves concentration, and supports mental and physical health.

The 20-20-20 rule

Every 20 minutes, look at something at least 20 feet away for at least 20 seconds. This gives your eyes a rest and can reduce digital eye strain and dryness.

Short breaks

Every 1–2 hours, step away from your screen for 5–10 minutes. Stand, stretch, walk, or look out a window. It helps your posture, circulation, and focus.

Longer breaks

Build in screen-free periods: an hour before bed, parts of the weekend, or a full day occasionally. Use that time for exercise, reading (physical books), or time with people in person.

Sleep and screens

Blue light and mental stimulation from screens can make it harder to fall asleep. Avoid screens for at least 30–60 minutes before bed, or use night mode and lower brightness. Charge your phone outside the bedroom so you're not tempted to scroll at night.
