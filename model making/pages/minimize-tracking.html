Minimize Tracking

Many websites and advertisers track your behaviour across the internet. Reducing tracking limits how much of your activity is collected and used for ads or profiling.

How tracking works

  • Cookies: Small files that remember you. First-party cookies help the site work; third-party cookies are often used for advertising across sites.
  • Fingerprinting: Sites can infer who you are from browser type, screen size, fonts, and other details.
  • Accounts: When you're logged in, the provider can link your activity to your identity.

What you can do

  • Browser settings: Block third-party cookies. Use "Do Not Track" if your browser offers it (some sites respect it).
  • Private/incognito: Reduces persistence of cookies in that session but doesn't make you anonymous.
  • Privacy-focused browsers: Browsers like Firefox (with strict tracking protection), Brave, or Tor Browser reduce tracking by default.
  • Search engines: Consider DuckDuckGo, Startpage, or similar for search that doesn't profile you for ads.
  • Ad blockers: Block many tracking scripts and ads; use from trusted sources.

Complete anonymity online is difficult, but these steps significantly reduce how much you're tracked.
